About the Island

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific with an area of less than 500 square miles. Our diverse terrain includes sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior featuring a small, active volcano.

Home to approximately 20,000 indigenous Tanitians, our island has recently opened its doors to tourism while maintaining its authentic cultural heritage.

Shuttle Services & Pickup Points

Hotel Shuttle Service

  • • The four-star resort offers complimentary airport transfers for guests
  • • Most family hotels can arrange shared shuttle pickup — ask at check-in
  • • Shuttle schedules align with major flight arrivals and the weekly cruise ship dock
  • • Book shuttle service at least 24 hours in advance through your accommodation

Activity Pickup Locations

  • • Rainforest & Volcano tours depart from Merriton Landing square (8 AM daily)
  • • Fishing & snorkeling charters board at Yellow Leaf Bay marina
  • • Helicopter tours depart from the airport helipad
  • • Bus sightseeing tours pick up at major hotels on request — confirm when booking

Safety Reminder: Helmets are required by law when cycling. All rental vendors provide helmets with bike rentals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar, but many businesses also accept euros and yen. Major credit cards accepted.

Power outlets are 120 volts (US standard). No adapter needed for US devices.

Alcohol cannot be served between midnight and 9:00 AM. Drinking age is 18.

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. Very little English in rural areas.

Taniti has one hospital with many multilingual employees and several clinics throughout the island.

Violent crime is very rare. Some pickpocketing reported as tourism increases. Use normal precautions.

Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays. Many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on holidays, so visitors should plan accordingly.
